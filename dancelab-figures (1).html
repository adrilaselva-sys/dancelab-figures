Interactive Figures — STUK / DanceLab Experience Centre

Designing the DanceLab

Three diagrams mapping the spatial, technical, and conceptual architecture proposed for the DanceLab Experience Centre, Leuven. From the paper by Adriana Parente La Selva, commissioned by STUK, 2026.

Figure 1
The four zones

The DanceLab floor plan as four functional zones, each shifting between modes depending on the programming. Hover over each zone to read its description. The dashed arrows indicate the circulation logic between zones.

Hover over each zone to read its description. Dashed arrows indicate circulation between zones.

Figure 2
The Deep Space — intra-active environment

Cross-section of the Deep Space showing the relationship between the archived body (solid), the live visitor (dashed), and the effort/trace energy (amber) that both generate. The Holo-Pit — a recessed LED glass floor with a Holo-Gauze drop — appears at the front of the room as a distinct volumetric zone within the larger space. Based on Karen Barad's concept of intra-action: neither body pre-exists the encounter — both are constituted through it.

Hover over the archived body and visitor figures for descriptions of the Laban Effort and laser tracking systems.

Figure 3
The infrastructure stack

The technical pipeline from physical input to display surface, showing four layers: input devices, processing and interpretation, archive storage, and display surfaces. Data flows vertically; the middleware layer (TouchDesigner) is the routing hub connecting all other layers.

Hover over any component to read its description. Vertical arrows indicate data flow direction.
